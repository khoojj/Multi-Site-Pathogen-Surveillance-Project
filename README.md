# Multi-Site-Pathogen-Surveillance-Project
### Situation, Action, Outcome

### Project Management · Stakeholder Coordination · Evidence-Based Delivery

End-to-end delivery of a £150,000 multi-site research programme across 2 years, from proposal to publication, demonstrating team leadership, cross-institutional coordination, and delivery under real-world constraints.

---

## Situation

Led delivery of a £150,000-funded, multi-site pathogen surveillance programme spanning multiple countries (Malaysia, Thailand, UK), from initial proposal through to publication. The programme required coordinating field teams, laboratory processing, and reporting across institutions with different capabilities, timelines, and regulatory requirements.

## Action

Managed the programme end to end. Secured ethics approvals and regulatory sign-off across sites (Malaysia and UK). Recruited and managed a six-person research team covering field logistics, sample collection, PCR screening, cell culture isolation, and data recording. Scope delivered including 5 field-sampling excursions and 2 community engagment workshops, SOPs for field and laboratory workflows, completed data analysis and publication output (6 publications total). Managed internal (ie. resources0 and external risks (ie. weather, community acceptance).
Ran weekly progress meetings with the internal team, tracking action points and assigning tasks to keep work moving between sites. Coordinated stakeholders including funders, collaborating institutions, and government agencies. Built the sample workflow and tracking system that kept fieldwork, laboratory processing, and reporting aligned as conditions changed across sites. Made scope and priority calls when timelines or resources came under pressure.

## Outcome

Delivered the programme on time and within budget, from concept to peer-reviewed publication. The coordination system and workflows built for the project remained usable beyond its original scope. Output presented in >5 international conferences. The work contributed to broader funding applications totalling approximately £200,000.

## Relevance

This project demonstrates end-to-end project ownership: planning, stakeholder management, team leadership, resource allocation, and delivery under real-world constraints, skills directly transferable to project management and coordination roles in commercial environments.
